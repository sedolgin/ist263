I loved journalism before I could properly comprehend the term and am working to build a future doing what I love.

I am graduating from the S.I. Newhouse School of Public Communications and the School of Information Studies at Syracuse University in May with a dual degree in newspaper and online journalism, and information management and technology with a concentration in data analytics.

I am the executive editor of Jerk Magazine, the premiere student-run arts and culture magazine, and work as the special projects lead producer for The NewsHouse, the communication school’s digital news site. The past two years marked a transition from my experience in news writing and gave me the opportunity to focus on mentoring underclassmen in the journalism school and polishing my skills in SEO and data visualization.

Outside of school, I can be found in downtown Syracuse interning at Central Current, a startup nonprofit newsroom focused on highlighting local voices. Additionally, I love to freelance and take on everything from newsletter articles to tributes and feature pieces. I aspire to combine my data and reporting skills to enter a full-time position at which I can work on enterprise and solutions-oriented projects.
